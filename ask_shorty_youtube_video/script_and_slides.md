## Ask Shorty – YouTube-Focused Video

Faceless explainer video for YouTube power users.

Total target length: **3–4 minutes**.

---

## Voiceover Script (Slide by Slide)

Speak naturally, conversational, calm/techy tone.

### Slide 1 – Hook (5–10s)

**Visual**: 
- Dark navy background with faint YouTube player silhouette.
- Simple animation: YouTube player → mouse moves to bookmark bar → small cyan bookmarklet icon gets clicked → quick montage of library grid filling up → big cyan “ASK?” and question mark.

**On-screen text (short)**:
- Line 1: “You’ve watched hundreds of YouTube videos.”
- Line 2 (after 1–2s): “Finding that ONE detail again is impossible.”
- Big text build: “What if you could just ASK?”

**Voiceover**:
- “You’ve watched hundreds of YouTube videos. Somewhere in there someone said exactly what you need right now… and you’ll never find it again. What if you could just ask?”

---

### Slide 2 – Problem (20–30s)

**Visual**:
- Dark background.
- Left: YouTube search bar with query “docker postgres setup” → results only show titles/descriptions.
- Middle: Huge wall of transcript text scrolling by.
- Right: Transcript chopped into floating “chunks”; small details (names, numbers) fly off and fade.

**On-screen text (short bullets)**:
- “YouTube history is a black box”
- “Search only sees titles & descriptions”
- “Transcripts = unreadable walls of text”
- “Chunk-only RAG drops important details”

**Voiceover**:
- “Right now YouTube history is a black box. Search only looks at titles and descriptions, not what was actually said. Transcripts exist but they’re huge walls of text. Even AI RAG tools chunk them and hope for the best. Important details—names, numbers, versions, explanations—get buried and lost.”

---

### Slide 3 – What Ask Shorty Is (25–35s)

**Visual**:
- Center: Simple card labeled “Ask Shorty”.
- Behind it, icons: YouTube logo, transcript document, Shorty card, question bubbles.
- Animation: Bookmarklet icon → video card flies into a “Library” panel.

**On-screen text**:
- Title: “Ask Shorty: search your YouTube brain”
- Subtext: “Bookmarklet → Shorty → Ask everything”

**Voiceover**:
- “Ask Shorty makes your YouTube history instantly queryable. You use a bookmarklet while you’re watching, it grabs the video, and processes the transcript into searchable chunks plus a Shorty. Then it shows up in your library. Now you can ask anything across your entire collection: ‘Where did I hear X?’ ‘What did they say about Y?’ and get precise answers with context.”

---

### Slide 4 – Workflow Overview (25–35s)

**Visual**:
- Horizontal flow diagram in cyan on dark background:
  - Icon 1: Play button with label “Watch”.
  - Icon 2: Bookmark icon with label “Bookmarklet”.
  - Icon 3: Library panel with label “Library”.
  - Icon 4: Search box with label “Ask”.
  - Icon 5: Answer card with label “Answer”.
- Simple wipe animation: each step appears in sequence with a connecting arrow.

**On-screen text (under diagram)**:
- “Watch → Bookmarklet → Library → Ask → Answer”

**Voiceover**:
- “The workflow is simple: you watch a video and hit the bookmarklet. The transcript gets grabbed and stored. In the background, Ask Shorty builds a Shorty—that’s a dense machine brief—plus synthetic questions and entities. Everything gets indexed together. Your library shows all your videos, their Shorties, questions, and entities. The Ask UI searches everything at once. I’ve got about three thousand videos with transcripts working this way.”

## Visual Style Guide

- **Background**: Dark navy / near-black (#040814 to #050B11).
- **Accent color**: Cyan `#00D4FF` for highlights, buttons, arrows, and key labels.
- **Font**: Clean sans-serif such as **Inter**, **SF Pro**, **Roboto**, or **Montserrat**.
- **Animations**: Keep it simple and professional:
  - Fades in/out.
  - Horizontal wipes.
  - Scale/zoom at 102–105%.
  - Build bullets one at a time where helpful.
- **Overall feel**: Calm, technical, not hypey. Think “dev tool demo”, not “launch trailer”.

## How to Turn This into a PowerPoint

1. **Create the presentation**
   - Open PowerPoint and create a new presentation.
   - Set slide size to **16:9**.
   - Set theme colors: dark navy background, cyan `#00D4FF` accent.
   - Set a single sans-serif font for all text.

2. **Slides**
   - Create **8 slides**, one per section above.
   - For each slide:
     - Copy the **On-screen text** into text boxes.
     - Recreate the **Visual** layout (use your two saved screenshots for the Library and Ask UI slides).
     - Add simple **Animations** only where called out.

3. **Timings**
   - Record your voiceover using PowerPoint’s “Record Slide Show” feature.
   - Use the **Timing Sheet** above to guide when you advance slides.
   - Adjust slide durations after recording, if needed, so visuals line up with your lines.

4. **Export**
   - When satisfied, export from PowerPoint:
     - `File → Export → Create a Video` → Full HD (1080p).
     - This gives you an **MP4** that you can bring into CapCut for final edits.
